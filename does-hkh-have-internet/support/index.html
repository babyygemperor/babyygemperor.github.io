IP Address of your router Experimental
Sometimes it helps setting a static IP Address for your router, than letting it dynamically choose one.
Try changing your router settings to these:
  1. Login to your router. (Normally it is 192.168.0.1 or 192.168.1.1)
  2. Set "Connection Type" to "Static IP"
  3. Use these values
    • IP Address:           192.168.xx.yy
    • Subnet Mask:        255.255.255.0
    • Default Gateway: 192.168.xx.1
    • Primary DNS:        1.1.1.1          Or any of your choice
    • Secondary DNS:    192.168.xx.1  Or any of your choice

    xx: here is {10, 11, 12, 20, 21, 22, 23}
      This is decided by where your apartment is located.
      The 10s place is decided by where your apartment is located. {NorthWing: 1, EastWing: 2}
      The 1s place is decided by which floor you're on. {GroundFloor: 0, FirstFloor: 1, SecondFloor: 2, ThirdFloor: 3}
      For example, Apartments 101 - 126 have the IP Address of 192.168.12.yy and the default gateway 192.168.12.1

    yy: here is any number in [2, 254], try any random number to get an unused address.
